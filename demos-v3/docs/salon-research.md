# The Salon — Research Notes

## Bulgari (bulgari.com)
- Hero: Full-bleed video, warm gold tones, cinematic
- Language: "Infinite Transformations", "Icons to Mix and Match" — emotional, not technical
- Typography: Large serif headings, warm palette
- Service: "Book an appointment" prominent, personal shopping feel
- Color: Rich golds, warm creams, deep blacks but always warm-toned
- Layout: Full-width hero sections, editorial storytelling approach

## Boodles (boodles.com)
- WARM British luxury — "expert, friendly and bespoke service"
- Language: "Start your love story", "cherished for generations", "designed to be worn and loved"
- Service-first: "BOOK AN APPOINTMENT", "Service like no other", "We are here to help"
- Trust signals: Complimentary delivery, beautifully packaged, expert courier
- Tone: Approachable, friendly, personal — NOT cold or corporate
- Collections named emotionally: "Play of Light", "Raindance"
- CTA: "Find a Boodles showroom" — inviting, not commanding
- Layout: Large hero carousel, warm photography, collection stories

## Key Takeaways for The Salon
1. WARM LANGUAGE: "love story", "cherished", "friendly", "bespoke service"
2. SERVICE FIRST: Appointment booking, personal advisors, expert help
3. TRUST SIGNALS: Delivery, packaging, service promises
4. EMOTIONAL NAMING: Collections have feelings, not just descriptions
5. PHOTOGRAPHY: Warm-toned, lifestyle, on-body, natural light
6. ROUNDED/SOFT: Boodles uses soft edges, warm backgrounds
7. PERSONAL TOUCH: "We are here to help" — conversational, not transactional

## Design Decisions
- Background: #FDF5EE (warm linen) — warmer than Gallery's cream
- Text: #2C2420 (warm dark brown) — NOT black
- Accent: #B8860B (dark goldenrod) — warmer than Vault's gold
- Corners: 12-16px rounded everywhere
- Shadows: rgba(44,36,32,0.08) — warm, not cold gray
- Fonts: Cormorant Garamond (headings), Lora (body), Inter (UI)
- Chat: Persistent concierge widget — Sophie, personal advisor
