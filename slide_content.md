## Slide 5: V3.2 Framework Change Log — The Path to 10/10

The framework assignments evolved through 4 iterations to achieve complete uniqueness:

| Version | Change | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| V2 (Initial) | All 10 concepts drafted | 2 framework duplicates identified |
| V3 (PR #4) | 6 framework swaps across concepts | Reduced to 1 duplicate (Gatsby) |
| V3.1 (PR #5) | Added checkout flow to Atelier | Non-framework fix |
| V3.2 (PR #6) | Concept 10: Gatsby → SolidStart | Final duplicate resolved — 10/10 |

The final change: Modern Maison moved from Gatsby to SolidStart because SolidStart's fine-grained reactivity without virtual DOM overhead perfectly serves the "balanced masterpiece" concept that needs both heritage gravitas and digital-native performance.

## Slide 7: Why Framework Diversity Matters for Vault-Maison

Framework diversity is not an academic exercise — it serves three strategic purposes:

1. **Proof of Concept Depth:** Each concept is architecturally viable, not just a visual mockup. The framework choice proves the interaction pattern can actually be built.

2. **Risk Mitigation:** If one framework falls out of favor or has a critical vulnerability, 9 other proven alternatives exist within the Vault-Maison playbook.

3. **Team Flexibility:** Different engineering teams have different strengths. A Vue-native team can build The Observatory (Nuxt.js). A React-native team can build The Vault (Next.js). A performance-obsessed team can build The Minimal Machine (Vanilla JS).

The technology layer is the foundation that makes each concept's signature interaction possible — from The Vault's biometric reveal (Framer Motion) to The Immersive Theater's WebGL constellation (React Three Fiber).
