# Research Notes - Luxury Jewelry Market Intelligence

- Grand View Research: $49.1B (2024), projected $82.1B by 2030, CAGR 8.7%
- Coherent Market Insights: $61.87B (2026), projected $108.12B by 2033
- Total global jewelry market: $349.5B (2024), expected $546.85B by 2033, CAGR 5.10%
- Lab-grown diamond market: >$29B (2025), projected $62.42B by 2035, CAGR 6.24%

## Bain Luxury Study 2024 Key Findings
- Personal luxury goods market: €363B in 2024, 2% decline vs 2023
- Only ~1/3 of brands experienced growth in 2024 (vs 95% in 2021-22, 65% in 2023)
- Luxury customer base shrank by ~50M people over last two years
- Jewelry held strong, especially haute joaillerie segment
- Gen Z advocacy for luxury brands continued to decline
- Top customers account for larger share of purchases
- Secondhand market gained traction, strong momentum in jewelry
- NPS for luxury industry 25-30 points lower among Gen Z vs millennials

## Digital Commerce Data
- Jewelry ecommerce conversion rate: 1.19% (2026) - PicupMedia
- Digital channels: 25-30% of total jewelry purchases
- Mobile commerce: >60% of online jewelry transactions
- Average order value jewelry ecommerce: $180
- Return rate: 20%

- McKinsey State of Fashion 2026: jewelry predicted fastest-growing fashion category through 2028

## Sustainability
- Consumers increasingly prioritize ethical sourcing

- Successful brands avoid comparative messaging vs natural

## BCG Luxury Report 2025 Key Findings
- Top-tier clients: avg annual luxury spend ~€360K, rising to ~€500K incl. cars/wellness
- Minimum spend threshold: >€50K
- 65% of top-tier clients feel overwhelmed by brands' over-communication and lack of personalization
- 80% prefer exclusive, dedicated spaces with privacy, calm, high-touch service
- 89% rate craftsmanship and product quality as top drivers of value
- 70% of potential top-tier clients are missed by brands (fall outside static definitions)
- Health-as-wealth mindset: wellness/longevity, beauty, interior design fastest acceleration (10% increase predicted next 18 months)
- Top-tier clients engage with up to 57 brands, receive 40-60 brand outreach instances monthly

## Jewelry Ecommerce 2026 Data (PicupMedia)
- Global jewelry market: $348B in 2025
- Online jewelry sales: $46.1B, compounding at 13.8% annually
- Jewelry ecommerce conversion rate: 1.19% (lowest of any retail category)
- 60% of online jewelry buyers hesitate when they cannot see piece worn in context
- Independent retailers: 42% market share (2020) dropped to 35%
- 28% of Gen Z jewelry discovery via social platforms
- Gen Z and Millennials drive 70% of online luxury jewelry purchases

## Scarcity Psychology (Psyche.co)
- Scarcity marketing: when luxury symbols widely replicated, exclusivity suffers (status dilution)
- Excessive/artificial scarcity leads to consumer skepticism, perceived manipulation, reactance

## White Space Psychology
- Heavy white space perceived as luxurious, high-end, sophisticated
- Reduces cognitive load, creates calm and clarity
- Direct correlation between white space and perceived luxury

## High-Ticket Online Purchase Barriers (Immerss, Jan 2026)
- Luxury/jewelry conversion rate: 1.19% vs 3-4% general retail
- Fine jewelry: 0.8-1.5% conversion; Luxury handbags: 0.3-0.5%
- Engagement rings: 1-2% (higher due to intent)
- Luxury shoppers spend avg 11 days researching vs 22 min for fast fashion
- 58% of luxury shoppers research on mobile
- 67% visit retailer websites during purchase journey
- Four key barriers: Trust Gap, Information Asymmetry, Experience Mismatch, Confidence Deficit
- Video commerce achieves 20-30% conversion rates
- Video consultations: 1.5-3x higher AOV, significantly reduced returns

## Ring Concierge (Forbes, Jan 2026)
